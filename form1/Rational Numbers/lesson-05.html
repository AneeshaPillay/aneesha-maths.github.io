Lesson 5: Rational Numbers

Learning Outcome

At the end of this lesson, you should be able to identify and represent rational numbers in different forms.

What are rational numbers?

A rational number is any number that can be written in the form:

\(\frac{a}{b}\), where \(a\) and \(b\) are integers and \(b \neq 0\).

This means rational numbers include:

  • Integers
  • Fractions
  • Terminating decimals

Connecting integers, fractions, and decimals

  • Every integer is a rational number. Example: \(5 = \frac{5}{1}\)
  • Every fraction is a rational number. Example: \(\frac{3}{4}\)
  • Every terminating decimal is a rational number. Example: \(0.6 = \frac{3}{5}\)
