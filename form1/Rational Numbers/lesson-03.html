Lesson 3: Positive and Negative Fractions

Learning Outcome

At the end of this lesson, you should be able to identify, represent, and compare positive and negative fractions.

What are fractions?

A fraction represents a part of a whole and is written in the form:

\(\frac{a}{b}\), where \(a\) and \(b\) are integers and \(b \neq 0\).

Examples:

  • \(\frac{1}{2}\)
  • \(\frac{3}{4}\)
  • \(\frac{5}{6}\)

Positive and negative fractions

  • A positive fraction has a positive value. Example: \(\frac{3}{5}\)
  • A negative fraction has a negative value. Example: \(-\frac{3}{5}\)

A negative sign can be written in different ways:

  • \(-\frac{2}{7}\)
  • \(\frac{-2}{7}\)

All of these represent the same value.

Fractions on a number line

Fractions can be placed on a number line just like integers.

  • Positive fractions are to the right of zero.
  • Negative fractions are to the left of zero.

Example order:

\(-\frac{3}{4} < -\frac{1}{4} < 0 < \frac{1}{2} < \frac{3}{4}\)

Comparing positive and negative fractions

  • Any positive fraction is greater than any negative fraction.
  • Among negative fractions, the one closer to zero is greater.

Examples:

  • \(\frac{2}{3} > -\frac{2}{3}\)
  • \(-\frac{1}{5} > -\frac{4}{5}\)

Common Mistakes

Mistake: Thinking \(-\frac{3}{4}\) is greater than \(-\frac{1}{4}\) because 3 is greater than 1.

Correct understanding: \(-\frac{1}{4}\) is greater because it is closer to zero.

Mini Practice

  1. State whether each fraction is positive or negative: \(-\frac{5}{6}, \frac{3}{8}\)
  2. Arrange \(-\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{4}, -\frac{3}{4}\) in ascending order.
  3. Which is greater: \(-\frac{2}{5}\) or \(-\frac{4}{5}\)? Explain.
