Lesson 2.1: Factors, Prime Factors & Highest Common Factor (HCF)

Learning Outcome

At the end of this lesson, you should be able to list factors of a number, identify prime and composite numbers, write numbers as prime factors, and find the Highest Common Factor (HCF).

What are factors?

A factor of a number is a whole number that divides the number exactly, with no remainder.

Example:

Factors of \(12\) are:

\(1,\ 2,\ 3,\ 4,\ 6,\ 12\)

Factors usually come in pairs.

\(12 = 1\times12,\ 2\times6,\ 3\times4\)

Prime numbers and composite numbers

A prime number has exactly two factors: \(1\) and itself.

A composite number has more than two factors.

  • Prime numbers: \(2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, \dots\)
  • Composite numbers: \(4, 6, 8, 9, 10, 12, \dots\)

Important: The number \(1\) is neither prime nor composite.

Prime factorisation

Prime factorisation means writing a number as a product of prime numbers only.

Example 1:

\(24 = 2\times2\times2\times3 = 2^3 \times 3\)

Example 2:

\(60 = 2\times2\times3\times5 = 2^2 \times 3 \times 5\)

Reminder: Always stop when all factors are prime.

Highest Common Factor (HCF)

The Highest Common Factor (HCF) is the greatest number that is a factor of all the given numbers.

Example: Find the HCF of \(18\) and \(24\).

Factors of \(18\): \(1,2,3,6,9,18\)

Factors of \(24\): \(1,2,3,4,6,8,12,24\)

Common factors: \(1,2,3,6\)

HCF = \(6\)

HCF using prime factorisation

Steps:

  1. Write each number as a product of prime factors.
  2. Select the prime factors common to all numbers.
  3. Use the lowest power of each common prime factor.
  4. Multiply them to find the HCF.

Example: Find the HCF of \(36\) and \(60\).

\(36 = 2^2 \times 3^2\)

\(60 = 2^2 \times 3 \times 5\)

Common prime factors: \(2^2\) and \(3\)

HCF = \(2^2 \times 3 = 12\)

HCF using the Ladder Method

The ladder method is a step-by-step method that uses common prime numbers to find the HCF.

Steps:

  1. Write the numbers side by side.
  2. Divide them by the same prime number.
  3. Continue dividing while both numbers can be divided.
  4. Stop when no common prime factor remains.
  5. Multiply the numbers on the left to get the HCF.

Example: Find the HCF of \(36\) and \(60\).

2 | 36   60
2 | 18   30
3 |  9   15
  |  3    5

Common prime numbers used: \(2, 2, 3\)

HCF = \(2 \times 2 \times 3 = 12\)

Try These

1. List all factors of \(20\).

2. Is \(29\) a prime number?

3. Write the prime factorisation of \(84\).

4. Find the HCF of \(24\) and \(36\).

5. Find the HCF of \(30,\ 45,\ 60\).

Show Answers

1. \(1,2,4,5,10,20\)

2. Yes, \(29\) is prime.

3. \(84 = 2^2 \times 3 \times 7\)

4. HCF \(= 12\)

5. HCF \(= 15\)
