Challenge

16. Use \( \text{HCF}\times\text{LCM} = a\times b\). So \(8 \times 96 = 768\). Other number \(= 768 \div 24 = 32\).

17. LCM\((6,8)=24\). But \(24\) is a multiple of \(12\). Next common multiple is \(48\), which is also a multiple of \(12\). Next is \(72\), also multiple of \(12\). In fact every common multiple of \(6\) and \(8\) is a multiple of \(24\), hence also a multiple of \(12\). So no such number exists.

18. LCM\((9,12)=36\). Multiples of \(36\) less than \(200\): \(36, 72, 108, 144, 180\).
