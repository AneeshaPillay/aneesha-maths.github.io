1) Square Numbers

A square number is a number obtained by multiplying an integer by itself.

Definition: If a number is n, then its square is n² = n × n.
Example: 7² = 7 × 7 = 49

Important idea: Negative Numbers

When we square a negative number, the answer becomes positive:
  • (-5)² = (-5) × (-5) = 25
  • But -5² means -(5²) = -25 (no bracket).
Tip: Use brackets for negative numbers before squaring.

2) Square Roots

The square root of a number is the value that gives the number when squared.

Definition: √a means the positive number whose square is a.
Example: √49 = 7 because 7² = 49.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Find 12².
Solution: 12² = 12 × 12 = 144
Example 2: Find √169.
Solution: 169 is 13², so √169 = 13
Example 3: Evaluate (-8)² and -8².
Solution:
  • (-8)² = (-8)(-8) = 64
  • -8² = -(8²) = -64
Example 4: Simplify: √(81) + √(16)
Solution: √81 = 9 and √16 = 4, so 9 + 4 = 13

3) Perfect Squares vs Not Perfect Squares

A perfect square is a number that is the square of an integer (like 36 = 6²). Numbers like 20 or 50 are not perfect squares, so their square roots are not whole numbers.

Quick check: If a number is between two consecutive squares, its square root is between those integers.
Example: 49 < 60 < 64 → 7² < 60 < 8² → √60 is between 7 and 8.
Extra: Estimating a square root optional

To estimate √N, find the two perfect squares around N.

Example: Estimate √50.
49 = 7² and 64 = 8², so √50 is between 7 and 8, and closer to 7 (because 50 is close to 49).

Quick Summary

  • Square: n² = n × n
  • Square root: √a is the positive number that squares to a
  • Perfect squares: 1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, ...
  • Brackets matter: (-5)² ≠ -5²
