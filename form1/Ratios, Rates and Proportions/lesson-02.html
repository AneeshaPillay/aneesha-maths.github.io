Lesson 4.2: Rates

Learning Outcome

At the end of this lesson, you should be able to understand the meaning of rate, calculate rates, and solve simple problems involving rates.

What is a rate?

A rate compares two quantities with different units.

Examples of rates:

  • km per hour (km/h)
  • RM per kilogram (RM/kg)
  • litres per minute (L/min)
  • words per minute

Example: If a car travels 120 km in 2 hours, the rate is: \[ 120 \div 2 = 60 \text{ km/h} \]

Difference between ratio and rate

  • Ratio: compares quantities of the same unit
  • Rate: compares quantities of different units

Example:

  • Ratio of boys to girls → same unit (people)
  • Speed → distance per time (km per hour)

Calculating a rate

To calculate a rate:

  1. Write the quantities clearly
  2. Divide the first quantity by the second
  3. State the correct units

Example 1: A cyclist travels 45 km in 3 hours.

Rate \(= 45 \div 3 = 15 \text{ km/h}\)

Example 2: 5 kg of rice costs RM20.

Rate \(= 20 \div 5 = \text{RM}4 \text{ per kg}\)

Unit rates

A unit rate is a rate where the second quantity is 1 unit.

Examples:

  • RM6 per 1 kg
  • 80 km per 1 hour
  • 30 pages per 1 day

Example: 12 notebooks cost RM36.

Unit rate \(= 36 \div 12 = \text{RM}3\) per notebook.

Solving problems using rates

Example: A machine produces 90 bottles in 6 minutes.

Production rate \(= 90 \div 6 = 15\) bottles per minute.

How many bottles are produced in 10 minutes?

\(15 \times 10 = 150\) bottles.

Try This

  1. A car travels 180 km in 3 hours. Find its speed.
  2. 8 kg of apples cost RM24. Find the cost per kg.
  3. A tap fills 60 litres of water in 5 minutes. Find the rate in litres per minute.
  4. A printer prints 240 pages in 6 minutes. How many pages are printed in 1 minute?
  5. If a person walks at 5 km/h, how far will the person walk in 4 hours?

Solutions

  1. \(180 \div 3 = 60\) km/h
  2. \(24 \div 8 = \text{RM}3\) per kg
  3. \(60 \div 5 = 12\) litres per minute
  4. \(240 \div 6 = 40\) pages per minute
  5. \(5 \times 4 = 20\) km
