Lesson 4.1: Ratios

Learning Outcome

At the end of this lesson, you should be able to write ratios, simplify ratios, compare quantities using ratios, and solve simple problems involving ratios.

What is a ratio?

A ratio compares two quantities of the same kind. It shows how many times one quantity is compared to another.

We can write a ratio in three ways:

  • \(a:b\)
  • \(a \text{ to } b\)
  • \(\frac{a}{b}\)

Example: If there are 3 girls and 2 boys, the ratio of girls to boys is: \[ 3:2 \]

Writing ratios

Follow this order carefully:

  • Ratio of A to B means \(A:B\)
  • Ratio of B to A means \(B:A\)

Example: In a class, there are 12 girls and 18 boys.

  • Girls : Boys \(= 12:18\)
  • Boys : Girls \(= 18:12\)

Simplifying ratios

To simplify a ratio, divide both numbers by their highest common factor (HCF).

Example: Simplify \(12:18\).

HCF of 12 and 18 is 6: \[ 12:18 = (12 \div 6):(18 \div 6) = 2:3 \]

Another example: Simplify \(20:35\). \[ 20:35 = (20 \div 5):(35 \div 5) = 4:7 \]

Ratios with different units

When comparing quantities with different units, you must convert them to the same unit first.

Example: Write the ratio \(2\text{ m} : 50\text{ cm}\).

Convert \(2\text{ m}\) to cm: \[ 2\text{ m} = 200\text{ cm} \] So the ratio is: \[ 200:50 = 4:1 \]

Example: Write the ratio \(1.5\text{ kg} : 600\text{ g}\).

Convert \(1.5\text{ kg}\) to g: \[ 1.5\text{ kg} = 1500\text{ g} \] So: \[ 1500:600 = 15:6 = 5:2 \]

Sharing using ratios

Ratios are often used to share a total amount.

Example: Share RM60 in the ratio \(2:3\).

Total parts \(= 2+3 = 5\).
Value of 1 part \(= 60 \div 5 = 12\).

  • First share \(= 2 \times 12 = \text{RM}24\)
  • Second share \(= 3 \times 12 = \text{RM}36\)

Try This

  1. Write the ratio of 8 to 12 in simplest form.
  2. In a bag, there are 15 red marbles and 10 blue marbles. Write the ratio of red to blue in simplest form.
  3. Write the ratio \(3\text{ m} : 150\text{ cm}\) in simplest form.
  4. Share 84 sweets in the ratio \(5:2\).
  5. In a class, the ratio of boys to girls is \(3:4\). If there are 24 boys, how many girls are there?

Solutions

  1. \(8:12 = 2:3\) (divide by 4)
  2. \(15:10 = 3:2\) (divide by 5)
  3. \(3\text{ m} : 150\text{ cm} = 300\text{ cm} : 150\text{ cm} = 2:1\)
  4. Total parts \(= 5+2 = 7\). One part \(= 84 \div 7 = 12\).
    Shares: \(5 \times 12 = 60\), \(2 \times 12 = 24\).
  5. Boys : Girls \(= 3:4\). If 24 boys represent 3 parts, then 1 part \(= 24 \div 3 = 8\).
    Girls \(= 4 \times 8 = 32\).
