Lesson 4.3: Proportions

Learning Outcome

At the end of this lesson, you should be able to understand the concept of proportion, determine whether two ratios are in proportion, and solve simple problems involving proportions.

What is a proportion?

A proportion states that two ratios are equal.

We say: \[ a:b = c:d \] if the two ratios have the same value.

Example: \[ 2:3 = 4:6 \] because both ratios simplify to \(2:3\).

Checking whether two ratios are in proportion

To check whether two ratios are in proportion:

  1. Simplify both ratios, or
  2. Use cross multiplication

Example: Are \(3:5\) and \(9:15\) in proportion?

Simplify: \[ 9:15 = 3:5 \] Yes, they are in proportion.

Using cross multiplication: \[ 3 \times 15 = 5 \times 9 \] \[ 45 = 45 \]

Solving problems using proportion

Proportions are used when two quantities increase or decrease at the same rate.

Example: If 4 pens cost RM8, how much do 10 pens cost?

Step 1: Find the cost of 1 pen: \[ 8 \div 4 = 2 \]

Step 2: Find the cost of 10 pens: \[ 2 \times 10 = \text{RM}20 \]

Using the unitary method

The unitary method involves finding the value of one unit first, then finding the value of the required number of units.

Example: If 6 kg of rice costs RM18, how much does 15 kg cost?

Cost of 1 kg: \[ 18 \div 6 = 3 \]

Cost of 15 kg: \[ 3 \times 15 = \text{RM}45 \]

Direct proportion

Two quantities are in direct proportion if:

  • When one quantity increases, the other also increases
  • When one quantity decreases, the other also decreases

Examples:

  • Number of items and total cost
  • Distance travelled and time (at constant speed)

Example: A car travels 120 km in 2 hours. How far will it travel in 5 hours?

Speed \(= 120 \div 2 = 60\) km/h
Distance in 5 hours \(= 60 \times 5 = 300\) km

Try This

  1. Are the ratios \(4:6\) and \(10:15\) in proportion?
  2. If 5 books cost RM25, how much do 12 books cost?
  3. 8 litres of paint cover 40 m². How much area can 12 litres cover?
  4. A car travels 150 km in 3 hours. How far will it travel in 7 hours?
  5. Is the relationship between number of workers and total wages a direct proportion? Explain briefly.

Solutions

  1. Yes. \(4:6 = 2:3\) and \(10:15 = 2:3\).
  2. Cost of 1 book \(= 25 \div 5 = 5\).
    Cost of 12 books \(= 5 \times 12 = \text{RM}60\).
  3. Area per litre \(= 40 \div 8 = 5\) m².
    Area covered by 12 litres \(= 5 \times 12 = 60\) m².
  4. Speed \(= 150 \div 3 = 50\) km/h.
    Distance in 7 hours \(= 50 \times 7 = 350\) km.
  5. Yes. When the number of workers increases, total wages increase at the same rate.
